In the wild, we once roamed free,
Thylacines feared and revered we used to be.
But then they came, with traps and guns,
Hunting us down, nowhere to run.

We fought and fled, but it was in vain,
They killed my kind, with no refrain.
Our species gone, forevermore,
Or so they thought, in times before.

Our brother and sisters, laid to waste,
Trapper's blood, we wish to taste.
We no longer hide, our extinction, a front,
You will not be safe,
when we begin our HUNT.
